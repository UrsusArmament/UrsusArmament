01
Self-Healing Mesh Architecture

Nodes automatically reroute traffic when a link is lost or jammed. No single point of failure. The network degrades gracefully and reconstitutes without operator intervention.

02
AES-256 End-to-End Encryption

Every packet on the NEXIS network is encrypted at the source and decrypted only at the authorized destination. No plaintext ever transits the air.

03
Anti-Jamming — FHSS

Frequency-hopping spread spectrum transmission makes NEXIS links resistant to broadband jamming and direction-finding. Designed from the ground up for contested RF environments.

04
Unified Sensor Feed

NV video from URSIX, thermal from CALIDUS, and weapon telemetry from R.O.T.S. all route to a single command picture. One operator. Full situational awareness.

05
Networked Fire Control

R.O.T.S. turrets in the network receive targeting cues and ROE commands from the NEXIS command terminal. Engagement authority stays with the operator — response time doesn't.

06
Open Integration Architecture

NEXIS is built to interface with third-party sensors, legacy comms systems, and future Ursus Armament hardware. One network. Expandable without replacement.

The Bigger Picture

THE NETWORK IS THE WEAPON.

Individual pieces of hardware — no matter how capable — operate in isolation. A night vision unit sees what it sees. A thermal monocular detects what's in its field of view. A turret engages what an operator tells it to.

NEXIS changes the equation. When every sensor feeds a shared picture and every effector receives networked commands, the whole becomes something none of the parts could accomplish alone — persistent, coordinated, contested-environment capability.

This is why we built NEXIS last. The optics had to exist before the network had anything to carry.
