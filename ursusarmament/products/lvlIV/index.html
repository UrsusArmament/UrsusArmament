01
NIJ Level IV Ballistic Protection

Rated to NIJ Level IV — protection against rifle threats up to 7.62×51mm NATO M80 ball at standard velocities. A meaningful step up from IIIA or III, designed to handle the threats that matter most in a rifle-threat environment.

02
Ceramic-Faced Construction

The ceramic strike face is engineered to shatter and defeat rifle projectiles on impact. Ceramic construction provides high ballistic performance at a weight profile that remains wearable for extended periods.

03
Rifle-Rated Threat Coverage

Level IV covers the threats that III does not. Where pistol-rated protection stops at handgun rounds, Level IV plates are engineered around rifle-caliber projectiles — the most common threat in serious defensive scenarios.

04
Multiple Cut Options

Planned availability in SAPI, Swimmer, and Shooters cuts to fit the widest range of plate carriers. Cut selection will be confirmed at launch based on testing outcomes and carrier compatibility.

05
USA Manufactured

Manufactured domestically, not sourced overseas. American-made ballistic products mean tighter quality control, consistent material sourcing, and no supply chain uncertainty. What we sell is what we'd wear.

06
Open Civilian Availability

Available to law enforcement, military, and law-abiding civilians where permitted. We believe Americans have the right to protect themselves with the same tools the threats use. Know your local laws before ordering.

Design Philosophy

STOP WHAT MATTERS.

A plate rated to NIJ IIIA or III is good protection. A plate rated to NIJ IV is a different category of protection. When the threat carries a rifle, handgun-rated armor is the wrong tool.

Ceramic construction is a deliberate choice — the same approach used across the military and law enforcement for decades. The ceramic face defeats the round. The backer catches the fragments. The wearer walks away.

We're still in testing. We won't sell you plates until we're confident in what they stop — and we'll publish the data when we're done.
