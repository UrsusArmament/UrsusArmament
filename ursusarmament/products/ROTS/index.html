R.O.T.S.

Dual-Weapon · Autonomous Profiles · Stand-Alone / Networked

The R.O.T.S. is Ursus Armament's remotely operated turret platform — purpose-built for persistent area denial, force multiplication, and remote engagement. Equipped with laser rangefinder, visible and thermal imaging, and configurable weapon rails for both lethal and non-lethal payloads. Operable stand-alone or integrated into a networked command architecture.

01
Laser Rangefinder (LRF)

Integrated LRF enables precise range-to-target data, feeding ballistic solutions and engagement parameters in real time — no external ranging device required.

02
Visible & Thermal Imaging (VIM / TIM)

Dual-sensor architecture provides continuous situational awareness in daylight, low-light, and total darkness. VIM and TIM feeds can be monitored independently or fused for operator clarity.

03
Dual Weapon Rail System

Two independent rail mounts allow simultaneous configuration of lethal and non-lethal payloads. Switch engagement type without swapping hardware or repositioning the platform.

04
Non-Lethal Capability

Purpose-built compatibility with deterrent munitions, directed energy devices, and aerosol dispensers. Configurable escalation of force without requiring a second platform.

05
Stand-Alone & Networked Operation

Deploy the R.O.T.S. as a fully self-contained unit or integrate it into a wider C2 network. Operates without external connectivity and reconnects automatically when available.

06
Persistent Custom Profiles

Define and store engagement rules, targeting parameters, ROE thresholds, and system behaviors as named profiles. Profiles survive power cycles and can be transferred across units in the same network.

Platform Philosophy

ONE PLATFORM. MULTIPLE MISSIONS.

A turret system that forces you to choose between lethal and non-lethal is a system built for a single scenario. The R.O.T.S. was designed around the reality that situations escalate and de-escalate unpredictably.

Dual weapon rails mean you configure for the mission, not the other way around. Persistent profiles mean your rules of engagement travel with the hardware, not with the operator. Stand-alone capability means it holds ground even when your network doesn't.

The R.O.T.S. is not a product we rushed to market. When it ships, it will be ready.
